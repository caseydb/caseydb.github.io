Why Emotional Intelligence Matters

When thinking about what intelligence means, it can be a natural reflex to think about intelligence quotient, or IQ. The reality though is that while that is important, it is no more important than the more recently talked about emotional intelligence, or EQ.

EQ is the ability to be aware of, evaluate and control emotions. There seems to be mixed research on whether it can be learned and strengthened, or whether it is something you are born with. What is clear is that everyone has it, but not everyone practices the awareness and control of it.


In nearly anything we do from life to family life, we interact with other people. Being able to look at things from their perspective and empathise with their needs, goes a long way in building relationships and increasing satisfaction in your pursuits be it personal or professional.

Having a high IQ is indeed attractive and helpful within your personal and professional life. However, having a high IQ is no guarantee of success.
