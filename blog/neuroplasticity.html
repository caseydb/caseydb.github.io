Neuroplasticity

Why Neuroplasticity Matters

The principles of neuroplasticity suggest that the brain is malleable and constantly changing. One of the benefits of this is the ability to rapidly improve at something by even thinking about the activity itself.


An example of how I might engage with neuroplasticity throughout my learning with Enspiral Dev Academy could be actively reflecting on practice. This could include writing functions in my mind and thinking about the way the arguments work together.


Research suggests that you can increase your neuroplasticity by creating multiple connections to an idea. The thought process is that when you activate your brain you fire up neurons to access this information. By having multiple connections to the idea, you increase the neurons that fire up and thus they cluster together to strong highway-like connections that deepen your understanding and ability to recall the information.


Sources: Positive Psychology by Courtney E. Ackerman, MA.
How Experience Changes Brain Plasticity by Kendra Cherry
6 Ways to Rewire Your Brain by Crystal Raypole

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is the state of mind where you believe you can become awesome at something. This counters the fixed mindset where you can feel there are limitations that you have no control over. Often through life you can land on false conclusions that you aren’t good enough at something. These doubts then result in action that reinforces the belief such as a lack of willingness to even try to improve. This is particularly relevant when studying to become a developer as there are a number of challenges that are outside of your comfort zone. Approaching them with a growth mindset is important.


To incorporate a growth mindset into my journey I am actively reflecting at the start and end of each day on the things that I feel I struggled with. I can then consciously move myself into a positive mindset that with practice and persistence I can get really good at those areas. I’ve already experienced this with JS functions. The great thing is that once you have a victory, you can use that a default example to give the growth mindset boost when you hit the next problem.


Sources: A Summary of Growth and Fixed Mindsets by Carol Dweck
Fixed vs. Growth: The Two Basic Mindsets That Shape Our Lives by Maria Popova
Growth Mindset by You Cubed
